## About me
My name is Max and welcome to my GitHub! You might know me as my bagel persona (though I've heard some people think it is a donut, but it is definitely a bagel) on various platorms. I'm a student studying cybersecurity at the Rochester Institute of Technology, where I am a member of our Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (CCDC) Team as well as member of the RITSEC Red Team. Here, you'll find various projects that I've been working on over the past couple of years, many of which I'm still contributing to. Feel free to follow me if you want to keep up with my coding journey!

## What am I currently working on?
### On GitHub
- **PWNBoard** ([link](https://www.github.com/max-49/pwnboard)): I currently maintain the most recent fork of PWNBoard, a dashboard for tracking beacon and credential callbacks during simulated Red Team engagements (such as red vs. blue cybersecurity competitions). If you use this for your red team engagements, feel free to open an issue with any problems you run into or leave a star if you found it helpful! Pull requests are also welcome.
- **ZeroPAM** ([link](https://www.github.com/max-49/zeropam)): Linux PAM shim for capturing plaintext credentials during simulated red team engagements (red vs. blue cybersecurity competitions). Applies a patch to the PAM source code for various Linux distributions (compiled dynamically using Docker containers) that allows for persistence on Linux machines during competitions.
- **RIT CCDC Competition Resources** ([link](https://www.github.com/CCDC-RIT)): As a member of the CCDC team, I work on our scripts and other resources to allow for automated auditing, backups, and system hardening of machines in a simulated enterprise environment during competitions. My focus recently has been on utilizing and securing the service Teleport, a secure Infrastructure Access Platform that secures access to servers, databases, and applications.
